About Me

I’m Charisma Anantharaman, a Healthcare Data Science graduate student at the University of Southern California with a strong foundation in machine learning, clinical informatics, and data engineering. I’m passionate about applying analytics to improve patient outcomes, advance medical technology, and build scalable data solutions.

I have experience in predictive modeling, similarity-matching algorithms for radiation therapy planning, and AI-assisted pathogen detection systems. As a Software Engineer at Innoventes Technologies, I developed machine learning models, designed data visualizations, evaluated algorithms, and implemented MLOps-driven model versioning systems.

My work spans phantom-generated imaging data evaluation, sequencing workflow development, music data analytics, and portable lab system pipelines for environmental pathogen detection. I work extensively with Python, SQL, AWS, Docker, Tableau, and scientific computing tools and enjoy solving meaningful healthcare problems.

Outside of academics, I volunteer with USC’s Viterbi Impact and previously taught Biology and English to underprivileged students through the Becoming I Foundation. I’m driven by curiosity, community impact, and the goal of building data-driven healthcare innovations.
